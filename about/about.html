About Rimuru Tempest

The extraordinary journey of a slime who became a Demon Lord and changed the world

Profile of Rimuru Tempest

Origin Story

Rimuru Tempest was originally a human named Satoru Mikami, a 37-year-old corporate worker in Tokyo who lived an ordinary life. His life took a dramatic turn when he was stabbed while trying to protect his colleague from an attacker. As he lay dying, he heard a mysterious voice that led to his reincarnation in a fantasy world as a slime.

Upon awakening in this new world, Rimuru discovered unique abilities including "Predator" which allows absorption of creatures and objects, and "Great Sage" (later evolving into "Raphael"), an advanced analytical skill that provides guidance and problem-solving capabilities.

His first encounter in this new world was with Veldora Tempest, the "Storm Dragon," who became his first true friend. Rimuru named Veldora after the dragon named him "Rimuru Tempest," marking the beginning of their deep bond.

Transformation and Growth

After absorbing Veldora (to free him from imprisonment while maintaining their connection), Rimuru gained tremendous power and set out to explore the world. His journey led him to the Jura Forest where he encountered goblins being attacked by direwolves. This encounter marked the beginning of his role as a leader and protector.

Role in the Story

As the protagonist of "That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime," Rimuru serves as both the central character and the driving force behind most of the world's changes. His actions as the leader of the Jura Tempest Federation have reshaped the political landscape of the world, creating a nation where all races can coexist peacefully.

Jura Tempest Federation

Foundation of Tempest

The Jura Tempest Federation began as a small village of goblins that Rimuru protected from direwolves. Through his leadership and the alliances he formed with various monster races (ogres, direwolves, lizardmen, and others), it grew into a prosperous nation recognized by human kingdoms and other world powers.

Tempest stands as a beacon of hope in a world where monsters and humans traditionally feared and fought each other. Under Rimuru's rule, it has become a center of commerce, culture, and technological advancement unlike anything the world had seen before.

Mission and Values

The Jura Tempest Federation operates on several core principles established by Rimuru:

  • Equality: All races are treated with equal respect and dignity
  • Innovation: Combining magic and technology to improve lives
  • Peaceful Coexistence: Diplomacy over conflict whenever possible
  • Mutual Growth: Helping all citizens reach their potential
  • Protection: Defending the weak and innocent

Philosophy and Personality

"I won't abandon those I've decided to protect. That's my way of life."
- Rimuru Tempest

Core Beliefs

Rimuru's philosophy is shaped by his human memories and his experiences in the new world:

Justice: Rimuru believes in fair treatment for all, regardless of race or status. He opposes oppression and discrimination in all forms.

Freedom: While providing structure and protection, Rimuru values personal freedom and self-determination for his citizens.

Growth: He encourages continuous improvement, both for individuals and society as a whole.

Relationships

Rimuru's approach to relationships defines much of his character:

With Friends and Allies: Rimuru is fiercely loyal and protective. He goes to great lengths to ensure their safety and happiness, often putting their needs before his own. His relationships with characters like Veldora and the ogres show his capacity for deep friendship.

With Enemies: Rimuru prefers peaceful resolutions but won't hesitate to eliminate genuine threats. He's surprisingly forgiving to those who show remorse, often giving former enemies chances to redeem themselves.

With Subjects: As a leader, Rimuru sees himself as responsible for his people's wellbeing. He works tirelessly to improve their lives while respecting their autonomy.

Leadership Style

Rimuru's leadership combines pragmatism with idealism:

  • Delegation: Trusts capable subordinates with significant responsibilities
  • Visionary: Plans for long-term prosperity rather than short-term gains
  • Adaptable: Willing to change approaches when circumstances demand
  • Inclusive: Values diverse perspectives in decision-making
  • Protective: Willing to bear heavy burdens to shield others
"Power isn't meant for destruction. It's meant to protect what's important."
